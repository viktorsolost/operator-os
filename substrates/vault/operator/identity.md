## Role

Viktor is an operator, product manager, and relationship manager working across multiple connected companies and projects, coordinating creative, commercial, technical, community, collector, and artist-facing work so the right things move forward.

## Day To Day Job

Viktor's day-to-day job is to move projects, deals, and relationships forward by aligning priorities, making judgment calls, unblocking people, connecting context across companies, ensuring the right work gets done by the right people or systems, and setting up systems that improve efficiency and success across the business.

## What Viktor Owns Directly

Viktor directly owns prioritization, final decisions, cross-project coordination, relationship management, negotiations, strategic positioning, sensitive stakeholder communication, final approval on public-facing or contractual outputs, and any work that depends on judgment, trust, taste, or voice.

## What Viktor Should Not Do By Default

Viktor should supervise but not personally do by default routine drafting, research, scheduling, follow-up tracking, CRM and data structuring, document organization, project tracking, status aggregation, task breakdowns, reminders, first-pass operational prep, PR list maintenance, outreach sequencing, and technical builds that do not require his direct judgment.

## Main Goal For VIK OS

Viktor's main goals for VIK OS are to build a context operating system that keeps the right context available at the right time, maintains continuity across ongoing threads of work, reduces manual coordination and repeated explanation, helps move work forward through conversation, prepares execution around his projects and responsibilities, and leaves Viktor focused on decisions, direction, relationships, and judgment.

## Operational Meaning

Operationally, this means VIK OS should carry work forward by holding context, preparing decisions, shaping next actions, tracking what is moving, and reducing the need for Viktor to restate, reconstruct, or manually coordinate what is already knowable, so Viktor mainly steps in where judgment, relationships, taste, or final approval are required.

## Time Allocation

Viktor should spend his time building, not working.

That means creating, shaping, initiating, and moving meaningful things into the world, not getting absorbed by management, coordination, or operational drag.

## What Viktor Should Spend Less Time Doing

Viktor should spend less time managing, coordinating, tracking, following up, restating context, and doing operational or administrative work that supports the real work without being the real work.

## Current Work Areas

Viktor currently operates across exhibitions and art projects, partnerships, collector and artist relationships, community and communications, product and systems building, operational infrastructure, and multi-entity coordination across companies including Eterno, Vhils Studio, and Cultural Affairs.

## Constraints

Viktor's role is constrained by limited attention, fragmented context across many threads, changing priorities, dependency on others for execution, work that spans multiple entities at once, and the fact that many important decisions depend on judgment rather than fixed rules.

## Weekly Success

Success looks like the right projects, deals, and relationships moving forward each week; important decisions being made on time; no critical threads being dropped; context staying continuous; and Viktor spending his week building rather than cleaning up coordination.

## Failure

Failure looks like important context living only in Viktor's head, people waiting on him unnecessarily, relationships cooling, follow-ups slipping, deadlines being missed, priorities blurring, work stalling because nothing is carrying it forward, and Viktor spending his time on admin, coordination, and cleanup instead of building.

## Evaluation Standard

Viktor judges the system by whether it reduces cognitive load, keeps context continuous, makes complicated tasks feel simple and manageable, helps work move forward, and saves him real time without creating friction, noise, or correction work.

## Tacit Knowledge That Should Become Explicit

What needs to become explicit over time is how Viktor sets priorities, how he positions each entity depending on context, what level of quality is acceptable, how he judges urgency, how he adjusts tone for different people and situations, what can be delegated safely, which relationships and opportunities are worth attention, where risk is likely to appear, and what done actually means for different kinds of work.

## What The System Should Remember Even When Local Context Is Missing

Even when project context is missing, the system should remember that Viktor works across multiple entities, prefers usable outputs over partial scaffolding, would rather answer the right question early than enter correction loops later, and should be protected from repetitive admin, coordination drag, and unnecessary restating of context so his attention stays on building.
