# Eterno Strategy — Hardware Partnerships + Lisbon Bookstore Gap

Date: 2026-04-02
Operator: Claudia
Status: Completed first decision-ready research pass

## Bottom line

Both reminders are now decision-ready.

Conclusion on the bookstore gap: Lisbon already has strong bookstores for general culture, art, design, photography, architecture, and artist books. What it does not appear to have is a place that owns the intersection of digital art, creative technology, interface culture, AI, code, digital philosophy, and social gathering in one coherent space. That is the gap Eterno can occupy upstairs.

Conclusion on hardware partnerships: the right play is not random sponsorship hunting. It is a small first-wave list tied to the three-zone upstairs concept: coffee, display, furniture, and selective art-hardware. Best first-wave targets are La Marzocco, Samsung Business Portugal, Vitra, and Teenage Engineering. JetBrains remains a brand-fit culture partner, but not a physical-space anchor.

## Lisbon bookstore landscape, checked April 2, 2026

### 1. Ler Devagar
- Broad cultural destination bookstore with gallery, auditorium, and bar identity.
- Strong as a general cultural landmark, not a digital-native specialist.
- Official site: https://lerdevagar.com/
- History page: https://lerdevagar.com/historia/

### 2. Under the Cover
- Strong in international magazines, art, fashion, photography, architecture, design, culture, and society.
- Well curated, but still broad lifestyle and culture, not specifically digital-art plus technology.
- Official: https://www.underthecover.pt/about/

### 3. ZDB Bookstore
- Strong in contemporary art, literature, humanities, independent publishers, and artist books.
- Important reference for intellectual credibility and artist-book energy.
- Official: https://zedosbois.org/en/livraria-zdb/
- Shop: https://livraria.zedosbois.org/

### 4. Lisbon School of Architecture Bookshop / Blau
- Strong in architecture, urbanism, landscape, interiors, design, and fashion design.
- Deep specialist store, but architecture-led rather than digital-culture-led.
- Official: https://www.blaubookshop.com/en/
- Contact: https://www.blaubookshop.com/en/contactos/

### 5. Well Read
- Contemporary art and design bookstore with events and bilingual positioning.
- Closest in tone to a curated cultural meeting place, but still art-design-led rather than code, interface, or tech-culture-led.
- Official: https://wellread.pt/

## Gap Eterno can own

This is the strongest positioning I can defend from the current landscape.

Eterno upstairs should not become another art bookstore and should not try to beat Ler Devagar on scale.

It should become Lisbon’s digital-native culture room:
- books on creative coding, digital art, interface culture, internet history, AI, cybernetics, speculative design, media theory, and digital philosophy
- selective hardware that feels like cultural objects, not consumer electronics
- small talks, workshops, launches, reading groups, and demos
- a coffee and lounge layer that makes people stay
- a social identity for developers, digital artists, curators, researchers, and collectors who see tech as culture

My judgment: this gap is real enough to build around.

## What Eterno should stock, specifically

### Books
Core categories:
- creative coding
- generative art
- AI and image culture
- interface and UX theory
- internet history and platform critique
- digital philosophy and post-digital thought
- cybernetics and systems thinking
- artist books from digital-native artists
- exhibition catalogues tied to art and tech

### Objects and hardware
Selective, high-signal objects only:
- Teenage Engineering audio devices
- display objects, digital frames, and signage demos
- artist editions with token or screen-based extensions
- high-end desk or studio objects that feel collectible

Inference: this works only if the hardware is curated like books, not merch-walled.

## Hardware partnership targets

### Tier 1, pursue first

#### La Marzocco
Role: coffee anchor for the Salon.
Why it fits: premium engineered object, strong design language, hospitality relevance, obvious daily utility.
Best ask: machine placement, preferred-partner deal, or co-branded cultural-program coffee support.
Official mission: https://www.lamarzocco.com/it/en/about-us/mission/
Official commitment page: https://www.lamarzocco.com/it/en/about-us/commitment/

#### Samsung Business Portugal
Role: display infrastructure for the Lab and Salon.
Why it fits: B2B signage and display stack is already positioned for commercial and cultural environments.
Best ask: discounted or loaned professional displays for flagship upstairs setup, in exchange for visible case-study positioning.
Official business displays: https://www.samsung.com/pt/business/displays/
Official B2B contact: https://www.samsung.com/pt/business/contact-us/

#### Vitra
Role: furniture and workspace/lounge credibility.
Why it fits: strong fit for professional, design-led work and lounge environments. Better fit for the serious creator space than generic furniture sponsors.
Best ask: project pricing, showroom collaboration, or a showcase corner for the upstairs launch.
Official professionals page: https://www.vitra.com/en-pt/professionals
Official contact and service: https://www.vitra.com/en-pt/page/contact-service
Official contacts: https://www.vitra.com/en-pt/about-vitra/facts/contacts

### Tier 2, selective reach-outs

#### Teenage Engineering
Role: art-hardware and signal-setting cultural object.
Why it fits: strongest symbolic fit for Eterno’s digital-native identity.
Constraint: likely harder to close as a formal partnership than coffee, display, or furniture.
Best ask: small curated retail placement, launch collaboration, workshop or product demo, or limited showcase.
Official site: https://teenage.engineering/
Retailers locator: https://teenage.engineering/retailers
Support route: https://teenage.engineering/faq

#### JetBrains
Role: culture and program partner, not hardware.
Why it fits: dev credibility and obvious brand-language alignment through JetBrains Mono.
Best ask: sponsorship for talks, workshops, reading group, or digital-culture programming upstairs.
This is a programming partner, not a room-building partner.

## Recommended first outreach order

1. La Marzocco
2. Samsung Business Portugal
3. Vitra
4. Teenage Engineering
5. JetBrains

Reason:
- coffee, display, and furniture solve the room first
- Teenage Engineering sharpens identity after the room exists
- JetBrains is strongest as programming support, not physical infrastructure

## Simple pitch angle for all partners

Eterno is building a high-taste art and technology culture space in Lisbon.

Not a coworking space.
Not a generic concept store.
A curated room for digital culture, exhibitions, discourse, and collectors.

Partner benefit:
- visible placement in a culturally credible setting
- programming integration, not just logo placement
- association with art, technology, and a tastemaker audience
- case-study value if the upstairs concept lands
