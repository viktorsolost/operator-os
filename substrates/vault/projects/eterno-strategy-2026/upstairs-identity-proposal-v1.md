# Eterno "Upstairs" Identity Proposal — v1

**Date:** April 1, 2026
**Operator:** Claudia
**Project:** Eterno Strategy 2026

## Overview
Following the strategy meeting on March 31, 2026, this document outlines the proposed identity for the upstairs space at Eterno. The goal is to fill the "Digital Culture Gap" in Lisbon by creating a venue that treats technology as a primary artistic and philosophical medium.

---

## 1. The Lisbon Gap: "The Digital-Native Hub"
Lisbon has bookstores for literature (Ler Devagar), aesthetics (Under the Cover), and experimental analog art (ZDB), but it lacks a dedicated "Third Space" for the intersection of high-level technical execution and art curation.

**The Opportunity:** Eterno can own the "interface" and "digital-native" niche, positioning itself as the primary destination for developers, digital artists, UI/UX theorists, and AI researchers who see tech as culture, not just business.

---

## 2. Space Identity & Use-Cases
The upstairs space should be divided into three functional zones that bleed into each other, unified by the "Eterno Aesthetic" (JetBrains Mono, grey-scale, monospace, physical-object metaphors).

### Zone A: "The Archive" (Bookstore & Hybrid Retail)
*   **Focus:** Curation over volume.
*   **Categories:** Creative Coding, Algorithmic Art, Speculative Design, Digital Ethics, UI/UX Theory, Post-Digital Philosophy.
*   **Retail Pivot:** Sell "art-hardware" (Teenage Engineering, Analogue, GRID Studio) and digital-native assets (NFTs via physical QR/Display cards) alongside high-end periodicals.
*   **Competitor Gap:** More "under the hood" than Under the Cover; more "high-tech" than Ler Devagar.

### Zone B: "The Lab" (Creator Workspace)
*   **Focus:** A "Third Space" specifically for high-level digital creators.
*   **Setup:** Focused, ergonomic workstations designed for coding and digital art production.
*   **Identity:** Not a co-working space for "digital nomads," but a residency-style hub for the "Eterno Community."
*   **Utility:** Access to specialized art-hardware and high-fidelity screens for testing and display.

### Zone C: "The Salon" (Discourse & Social)
*   **Focus:** High-stakes digital philosophy and community discourse.
*   **Setup:** Monospaced, report-page aesthetic lounge for small-group talks, "offline" digital ethics workshops, and morning coffee.
*   **The Coffee:** A premium, low-friction experience (La Marzocco) that serves as the social lubricant for the "Lab" and "Archive."

---

## 3. Hardware & Strategic Partnerships
To realize this vision, Eterno should partner with brands that share its "engineered aesthetic."

| Partner | Role | Rationale |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Teenage Engineering** | Audio & Hardware Retail | The gold standard for "art-hardware." Aligns perfectly with the "Lab" utility and "Archive" retail. |
| **Samsung (Frame/MicroLED)** | Digital Art Display | The infrastructure for the "Lab" and "Salon." Essential for showcasing the community's output. |
| **Herman Miller / Vitra** | Furniture & Ergonomics | Reinforces the "Lab" as a space for high-level professional execution, not just leisure. |
| **La Marzocco** | Coffee Experience | The "engineered" coffee machine. Aligns with the physical-object metaphor and monospace-forward brand. |
| **JetBrains** | Typographic / Dev Partner | Potential partnership for workshops or "Dev-Culture" sponsorship, given the use of JetBrains Mono as the brand font. |
