# Exhibition Glossary

## Purpose

Shared exhibition vocabulary for interpreting requests, documents, and project context.

## Terms

### Opening
The public opening moment or event. Often acts as a fixed anchor date that drives backwards planning.

### Installation
The physical setup period for works, space, signage, lighting, and supporting materials before opening.

### Deinstallation
The teardown period after the exhibition closes.

### Venue Requirements
Rules, constraints, and operational conditions imposed by the venue. These often affect installation, contracts, logistics, and approvals.

### Checklist
An execution-oriented list of setup or delivery items. Useful operationally, but not the same as a project plan or milestone structure.

### Milestone Structure
The durable high-level project sequence used to organize work. This should stay more stable than day-to-day task lists.

### Production Calendar
A time-based schedule mapping deadlines, dependencies, and key work periods. Usually derived from the opening date and installation window.

### Loan / Artwork Movement
Transport, handling, customs, courier, and condition-related movement of works. This is often a risk-heavy area.

### Wall Text / Labels
Finalized exhibition-facing text elements that usually depend on approvals, production deadlines, and venue formatting constraints.
